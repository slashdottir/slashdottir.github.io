Most establishments always have a back entrance. Keep staff don't need the front door experience, t hough we are programmatically capable of being part of one. This time I would not use the back entrance.

A handsome cartoonish eight foot tall animatronic gentleman stood outside the front door of the shop. It bowed deeply as I approached and with a smooth upsweep of his hand doffed his top hat saying, "Come on in, don't be shy. "Big sale today! "Step right up, come on in. he urged with a wink and a winning smile. "Welcome to Dan's Used Bots where Bots get a new lease on life!" it admonished as it it tapped the poster board with the tip of his cane. "Discount on repairs all through January" it advised as it tipped his hat in acknowledgment to a couple walking the other direction.

"Minor damage repaired free for new customers who join our newstream," it advised as I passed through the door. As I passed his activation zone, the salesbot relaxed into a neutral routine, ocasionally smiling and winking and nodding amiably.

Behind the counter sat the amiable, cantankerous old gentleman. Uncle Dan is just as likely as any, if no moreso, inclined to bend your ear with tales of great astrophysicial implication and expound upon the metallurigical manifestations of entropy in the windings of a pocket watch.

As such he is often tired, having exhausted himself earlier from all his endeavors to elicitate excitement from any and all willing to have their ears bent.

So it is best, for the sake of efficiency, to visit the shoppe in the later afternoon as I am doing here today.

I spied upon the sidewalk a city clock burnished soft green upon a pedestal which indicated the time was 4:20 exactly.
