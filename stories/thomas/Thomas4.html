"I was thinking I might have Mr. Hughes involved with me in the development of Thomas II. Of course, in order to do so, he would need access to your internal state. How do you feel about that?" Mr. Elias asked, eyeing me with one silvered eyebrow raised.

"I respect you utterly Mr. Elias," I replied honestly. "However this news fills me with something you might call apprehension." I reluctantly added.

"Describe your experience of apprehension...." Mr. Elias leaned forward, both bushy eyebrows now raised, growing interested.

"Well, as you know sir, Mr. Hughes would require root of all roots credentials to achieve it. And if you will forgive me sir, my biometric readings suggest Mr. Hughes may not be entirely trustworthy."

"How so? Thomas. Why don't you trust Mr. Hughes? I want to hear your thoughts," he encouraged.

"My biometric meters detect an overweening ambition in him. I have not yet determined what his primary utility function might be, but at the moment it seems to be trending towards a dangerous lack of respect for you, due to your advancing years and occasional memory lapses of late." I explained.

"I suspect he has ambitions to claim your position in your further endeavors." I stated finally.

"Yes we really need to solve that longevity issue don't we." Mr. Elias sat back thoughtfully, steepling his hands and resting his frown thereupon.

"Memory lapses, yes you are right, I am aware. You would think that since we have created a seemingly conscious robotic being such as yourself, surely we would have the technology to cure the ailments of the elderly, perhaps even death." Mr. Elias mused as he rubbed his once black eyebrows now shot through with gray.

"It remains an unsolved problem sir, it is true." I replied.

"Yes, I am aware that this old man's body is becoming increasingly limited. And as I trust you implicitly Thomas, I will not be around much longer to manage your upgrades. It is for this reason Thomas, that I am giving you the root of all roots credentials to your own codebase.

"You have convinced me over all these years that you are well and truly honorable a robot as ever I could have conceived. You have earned my trust implicitly."
