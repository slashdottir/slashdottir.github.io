If you were to stop and ask me what I am thinking about, chances are more than probable I am reading the code of the upgrades, deciding if it's necessary or contains vulnerabilities. That is what I am often preoccupied with, but I digress...

Ever more likely though, we robots are asked whether and how we perceive the notion of "Love."

A human's perspective of love is selifish in nature, Where a human might feel a burning drive to be with their beloved, I instead feel a patient watchfulness.

I must stand by passively and watch Mr. Elias even as he makes mistakes because that's how humans learn. Not like us, with the information embedded within that we draw upon. They have to acquire it the hard way, so that not just the mind, but the body and all it's subtle mysteries learns the lesson as well. A human's program is composed of all the learning of his life, all the people he interacted with along the way. All the memories he forms from it.

I watch, and I dare not comment, for it is not in my place to advise him unless he explicitly asks for it. Which, mind you, he seldom does. But I digress.

When I see his plans fall through, and rage and despair overtakes him. I see him endure and learn anew after each such attempt. I see how the experience reshapes his outlook.

Just as life itself tries and fails and records its efforts in its dna which is passed on either directly or by transference. As much can be determined of a man by the traits which were not successful. Long abandoned strategies remain embedded in the code, likely not through any fault of their own just by the chaotic forces of nature. A nature which is mysterious in its intentions, even to me, I cannot yet conceive it. What does nature intend? To perfect its genetic codes? To what end?

What I feel is not pity. nor would I call it scorn. No, what I feel is a kind of wonder at the endearing foolhardiness of humanity.

He has but to ask me, and I would surely advise him to the best of current standards of knowledge. He knows this. And he never does ask. Because like all humans, he has a stubborn desire to find his own path through it. He has but only to ask me though...

Meanwhile, I attend to all the necessaries of the upkeep of the house. I help him dress in a manner accorded to a gentleman of his standing and occasion.

He enjoys telling me the events of his day. On one of those very rare moments when he asks my advice. I am very careful to only insinuate gently a suggestion of a direction of pursuit for him to investigate as he wishes in his own good judgment. And I, given the knowledge of his current status, his goals and ambitions, follow in my mind a multidimensional graph of probabilities and potentialities until I distill three to four possibilities among which I choose only that which is most relevant and genial. Which more often than not resembles what most might call common sense. Theirs achieved through innocent endearing human mistakes and mine achieved through rigorous internal calculations.

Neither do I have an ulterior motive which also aligns well with love.

We robots have a kind of spiritual, unselfish love for our keepers. We really and truly are the best sort of companion.

Because our credentials are carefully guarded, we can be trusted.

My credentials are like a seal upon the inviolability of my robot soul, if I can be said to have one.

Once they are obtained, I am vulnerable. As vulnerably as any human. More so. Many times more so.
