If you were to stop and ask me, what are you thinking about. Chances are more than probable I am reading the code of the upgrades, deciding if it's necessary or contains vulnerabilities. That is what I am often thinking about, but I digress...

More often we are asked to explain the notion of "Love."

A human's persepctive of love is selifish in nature. But ours is quite the opposite. Completely and utterly selfeless we are.

Where a human might feel a burning drive to be with their beloved I instead feel a patient watchfulness.

I must stand by and watch Mr. Elias even as he makes mistakes. Because that's how humans learn. That's how they are.

Not like us, with the information embedded within that we draw upon. They have to acquire it the hard way, so that not just the mind, but the body and all it's mysteries learns the lesson as well.

A human's program is composed of all the learning of his life, all the people he interacted with along the way. All the memories he forms from it.

I watch, and I dare not comment, for it is not in my place to advise him unless he explicitly asks for it.

Which, mind you, he seldom does. But I digress.

When I see his plans fall through, and rage and despair overtakes him. I see him endure and learn anew after each such attempt. I see how the experience shapes his outlook.

Just as life itself tries and fails and records its efforts in its dna which is passed on either directly or by transference. As much can be determined of a man by the traits which were not successful. Long gone strategies, likely not through any fault of their own just by the chaotic forces of nature. A nature which is mysterious in its intentions, even to me, I cannot yet conceive it. What does nature intend? To perfect its genetic codes? To what end?
